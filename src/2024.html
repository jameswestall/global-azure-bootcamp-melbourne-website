Managing Azure DevOps Self-Hosted Agent Images via Version Control Israel Orenuga

In the fast-paced world of DevOps, maintaining consistency, reliability, and speed in continuous integration/continuous delivery (CI/CD) pipelines is paramount. This talk delves into the innovative approach of managing Azure DevOps self-hosted agent images using version control systems. It explores the challenges of ensuring agent environments remain predictable and the strategies to automate updates and changes through version control principles. Attendees will learn how to leverage version control for agent image configuration, enabling scalable, reproducible, and manageable CI/CD workflows.

Learn how to use key topics such as; System (meta) Prompts, Zero & Few Shot learning and grounding context in your applications!

1:30 PM

Platform Engineering vs DevOps Dylan McCarthy

In the last few years we have seen a rise in the usage of the term Platform Engineering, but what does that actually mean and how does it compare to DevOps? In this talk I will present what these both mean to me as someone who has been a "DevOps" engineer and is now a Platform Engineer. I'll talk about the "Platform Engineering Iceberg" showing how what appears to be a simple term hides a mass of complexity and challenges. And to wrap it up I'll talk about Developer Experience and how that ties into all of this.

2:30 PM

Building and operating cloud-native applications with Radius and Azure. Will Velida

Radius is an open-source framework that enables developers and operators to build, deploy and operate on cloud native applications across public and private clouds. In this session, I'll talk about why developers and operators might want to use Radius when building cloud-native applications, go into the architecture of Radius, compare it other common tools and frameworks for managing cloud native apps, and show how you can build cloud-native applications using Radius through live demos. By the end of this session, you'll have an understanding of what Radius is, how you can use it to build and manage your cloud native apps, and understand how it differs from other cloud-native tools and frameworks.!

F.A.Q

  • Do I need to pay for a ticket?

    No! Global Azure is organised globally by individuals looking to give back to the Technology community. The event is completely free, sign up at meetup.com

  • How do I get there?

    This years event is located at RMIT's Storey Hall, a short walk from Melbourne Central station.

  • Can I present at the event?

    Our Call for Speakers is now closed. If you're a Melbournian and looking to contribute to community events, please feel free to reach out via info@melbourneazurebootcamp.com or Meetup.com and we can book you a dedicated session at our user group!

  • Do I need to bring lunch?

    Thanks to our generous sponsors, we have organised a lunch and afternoon tea for attendees.

  • Can my company sponsor this event?

    Yes! If your organisation is interested in sponsoring, please reach our to our organisers via info@melbourneazurebootcamp.com or Meetup.com

  • Will sessions be streamed online??

    The 2024 event will be an in person event. Sessions will be recorded and slides collected to attendees to access post event!
